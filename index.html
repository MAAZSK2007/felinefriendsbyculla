About Us ‚Äì Feline Friends Byculla

Welcome to Feline Friends Byculla ‚Äì a heartfelt initiative born out of love, compassion, and a deep sense of responsibility towards the voiceless.

We are a small team of one ‚Äì a working student juggling college, work, and life ‚Äì with a big heart for street cats. Every two days, I feed 15 beautiful street cats in and around Byculla, Mumbai. These cats aren't just animals to me ‚Äî they are family. Each one has a name, a personality, and a story.

Some are playful kittens born under parked cars. Some are older cats who've survived harsh monsoons and scorching summers. One is a rescued Persian cat I found near my building, abandoned and struggling to breathe. Despite her respiratory issues, she still purrs softly and waits for food with hope in her eyes. Others have been treated and nursed back to health after injuries or infections ‚Äî and their recovery has only been possible through love, patience, and whatever little I could manage.

But the truth is, feeding 15 cats regularly on a student budget is tough. I buy whatever I can afford ‚Äî Purepet gravy, Whiskas pouches, and 1kg dry food packs ‚Äî and prepare meals despite having very little time. Some days I skip meals, but I can't bear to see their empty bowls.

Sadly, not everyone understands this bond. I face constant opposition from my housing society and neighbours who see these cats as a nuisance. But I carry on. Because these cats have no one else.

This website is my way of reaching out. If you've ever loved an animal, you'll understand. If you believe kindness matters, I hope you'll help.

Your donation ‚Äì big or small ‚Äì helps feed a hungry cat. It helps me buy medicine when they're sick. It gives these innocent lives a chance to feel safe, even if just for a day.

We've also added a gallery to show you the cats who've been treated and saved ‚Äì their photos speak louder than words.

Thank you for being here. Thank you for caring. Because of people like you, these cats still have hope.

Rescue Stories

Mickey's Rescue Story

It was monsoon season, and the rain was pouring heavily outside. We were just sitting down for dinner when we heard a faint sound‚Äîbarely audible over the rain. It was a kitten crying, but the sound was so soft that we weren't even sure at first. We looked out the window, but the heavy rain and darkness made it hard to see anything clearly. Still, something told us to go downstairs and check.

We followed the sound for a while, and after about 10 minutes‚Äîonce the rain had slowed down‚Äîwe found two tiny kittens. They looked exactly alike, same age, same pattern, completely soaked in muddy water. Their eyes were covered in dirt, and they were half-submerged in a water-filled pothole. It was heartbreaking.

My mom immediately picked them up and brought them home. We cleaned them gently and gave them some warm diluted milk (since regular milk isn't good for kittens, but we didn't have anything else at that moment). One was a female‚Äîshe was the one crying for help. The other, a male, was silent and barely breathing.

Since it was late at night, we couldn't take them to a vet. We kept them warm and fed the female kitten a little milk. But the male kitten wasn't responding much. Later that night, we noticed he had a severe worm infestation around his legs. We separated them to keep her safe, but sadly, by morning, the male kitten had passed away.

We focused all our care on the female kitten. We gave her proper kitten food, wet gravy, and boiled chicken. We named her Mickey. She was so lively and playful, and we instantly fell in love with her. We knew we couldn't let her go.

Now, Mickey is 3 years old‚Äîhappy, healthy, fully vaccinated, dewormed, and neutered. She lives with us and brings so much joy into our lives every day. From that rainy night to now, she's truly living her best life.

Drum Rescue

Since most people in my building know I care for cats, one day a friend came running to tell me about a kitten stuck inside a big drum. We had no idea how he ended up in there, but there was a bit of water at the bottom and he looked completely helpless.

We rushed to get him out. When we pulled him up, we saw scars and scratch marks all over his body and head‚Äîprobably from trying to climb out and falling back in over and over again.

And here's the crazy part‚Äîwe had always wished for a black kitten, and there he was, right in front of us.

We decided to keep him without a second thought. Today, he's 7 years old‚Äîvaccinated, dewormed, neutered, and living a good life. He's healthy, playful, and sometimes a bit aggressive with the local street cats‚Äîbut he's family now.

Critical Rescue

This was one of the most critical cases we've ever handled.

One of the stray cats we regularly feed showed up one day with a severe wound‚Äîthere was a deep hole in her leg. We immediately took her home, cleaned the wound with Betadine, and dressed it with bandages.

As days passed, the wound started getting worse instead of healing. So we took her to Parel BSPCA for proper treatment and tests. That's when we got the full picture‚Äîshe was suffering from jaundice, which we hadn't known, and also from a rare condition where the skin was detaching from the body and hanging loosely, almost like it was separating from the flesh underneath.

Despite the worsening condition, we admitted her and did everything possible. The bills added up to ‚Çπ7,000‚Äì‚Çπ8,000, but we didn't hold back.

Sadly, even with all that effort, she passed away at the hospital due to a heart attack, as the vets informed us.

We'll never forget her‚Äîand we carry that experience as a reminder of how precious and fragile these lives are.

The Little Visitor

In my area, I regularly feed many stray cats. One day, a small kitten showed up during feeding time, so we gave her food just like the others. She kept coming back every day for about a week, but slowly we noticed she was growing weaker and seemed faint.

Then, one day in 2025, she wandered into our building‚Äîand even came into our home on her own. We decided to keep her for a while and feed her properly. But soon, we noticed something alarming: she was urinating blood.

We quickly took some photos and rushed her to Parel BSPCA. The vets there prescribed NERI syrup, Liv-Vitol, and Amoxicillin. Thankfully, within just three days, she was doing much better.

We started looking for a permanent home for her‚Äîand by a stroke of luck, a friend of ours who lives on the same floor was looking to adopt a kitten. They loved how playful and beautiful she was, and happily welcomed her into their home.

Now, she's safe, healthy, and living right next door‚Äîso I still get to see her from time to time.

Bridge Rescue

One day, while we were near Mazgaon Bridge, we heard loud kitten cries coming from somewhere nearby. The sound echoed, and we couldn't figure out where it was coming from at first. After some searching, we finally spotted a tiny kitten peeking through a small opening halfway down the bridge's height. It was such a risky spot, and we were honestly scared to even get close.

We stopped a biker passing by and asked her for help. Thankfully, she managed to get the kitten down safely. I brought the kitten home, gave her food, and checked her condition‚Äîluckily, she seemed fine.

We spent the next two days trying to find her mother near the bridge and actually found a cat who looked exactly like her. But sadly, the mother didn't recognize or accept her. We left the kitten there for a day, hoping she might reunite with her mom, but the next day her health had worsened.

That's when we decided to keep her with us until we found someone to adopt her. A week later, one of my friends offered to give her a home. He later even adopted a male cat who looked just like her so she wouldn't feel lonely.

Today, she's 3 years old, and although I'm no longer in touch with that friend, I truly hope she and her companion are living a happy, healthy life.

The One Who Came Back

Near our building, there used to be a big clothing store with a wide open gate. A female cat had given birth to 3‚Äì4 kittens near the corner of the shop, so I placed a box there to give them some shelter. She used to stay inside it with her babies.

One day, I saw that the box had been kicked away‚Äîprobably by the shopkeepers since it was close to their entrance‚Äîand the poor kittens were lying out on the road. We didn't argue with them, but I quietly moved the box to a safer area.

The next day, the mother cat and all the kittens had vanished‚Äîexcept for one, who was found in a different area. We searched everywhere for the mother but never found her. I brought the kitten home to feed him, but since I already had cats, I wasn't allowed to keep another one inside.

So I started taking care of him outside my chawl, feeding him regularly and making sure he was okay. This was sometime in 2018, before COVID hit. One day, he disappeared too. I searched all over the building and nearby areas, but he was just gone.

Then, in 2020‚Äîduring the peak of the pandemic‚ÄîI saw him again. He was sleeping near the same clothing shop where his mother and siblings had once lived. I couldn't believe it. I tried to bring him home, but by then he had become very wild and aggressive. I knew he'd become a street cat, and I respected that.

Now it's 2025, and he's still around. I feed him whenever I see him‚Äîusually about three times a week, though he disappears often. Who knows where he roams? But I hope, truly, that he lives a long and safe life out there.

A Box of Hope

Everyone in my locality knows that I feed and care for stray cats. One day in 2018, someone abandoned two tiny kittens in a box near our gate. They cried non-stop for hours until we brought them inside, fed them, and gave them warmth.

By the next day, both kittens fell sick with severe diarrhea and vomiting. We treated them with Metrogyl and Amimeo syrups, and thankfully, within five days they recovered. They turned out to be full of mischief and play, bringing so much joy to our home.

We cared for them until we found them a loving home. Fortunately, a family in our village (Aurangabad) was looking to adopt, so we sent them there by train. I haven't received any updates since(2018), but I hope they are still living a happy life.
